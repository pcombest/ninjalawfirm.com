The shadow of a past Hungarian king kept following her down the Danube as she strolled the walk on the Buda side. She had a great view of the Parliament but was afraid to stop and allow the shadow to catch up. She decided to cross the suspension bridge with lion heads on it. For the first time, she noticed that the lions had no tongues.

She had a flashed vision of her tongue being cut off before the royal court. She turned around and the shadow of the Hungarian king (Was it Istvan?) bitch-slapped her and demanded her to go pick up her kids from her ex-husband's place. She started to run to do just that, but tripped and fell in the cold Duna. She swam momentarily and was relieved upon finding the water not to be as cold as she expected. She drew the attention of a boat going down the river blasting heavy-bass infused electronic music. The crew pulled her aboard the floating club and the dancers cheered.

She went to her ex-husband's to pick up her kids and argue about how little he pays in support.

8.23.10

The following is a conversation I recorded between Ol' Dirty Bastard and Clint Eastwood at a special screening in Los Angeles for the film The Bridges of Madison County. This is only a small portion of a much-longer four-hour-plus transcript. This as-yet-unseen transcript will serve as a guide to a 1,000-page novel I am currently writing that is tentatively entitled The Diaper of Big Baby Jesus. 

Ol' Dirty Bastard: Why are you dressed like a pilgrim? 

Clint Eastwood: A pilgrim? 

ODB: Yes, with that hat. Those shoes look like Thanksgiving shoes too. 

Eastwood: It's for a movie I'm doing. 

ODB: Why would you do a movie with those shoes? 

Eastwood: I'm trying to get into character. 

ODB: Are you playing a gay? 

Eastwood: A gay? 

ODB: To me they look like a gay's shoes. 

Eastwood: Why would I gaze at my shoes? 

ODB: No, a gay's shoes. 

Eastwood: I thought you said I look like a pilgrim. 

ODB: What's the difference? 

Eastwood: There's a big difference I think. 

ODB: You still like Dirty Harry? 

Eastwood: What do you mean? 

ODB: Do you like Dirty Harry? 

Eastwood: Sure I like him. He was a good character to play. 

ODB: When I was a boy I used to dress my sister up like Dirty Harry but instead I called her Dirty Mary. She was messed up. I remember on one of those days she was chasing me with a gun and I threw her into a woodpile. 

Eastwood: Why? 

ODB: Because she was taking it real. The bitch thought she was you. 

Eastwood: Oh yeah? 

ODB: And she got a big splinter in her foot. 

Eastwood: I bet that hurt. 

ODB: I'm sure it hurt but I had to remind Dirty Mary that she wasn't Dirty Harry, for real. 

Eastwood: Right. 

ODB: The oddest thing about it was that the splinter in her foot was in the shape of the Virgin Mary. 

Eastwood: Right. 

ODB: It looked just like the Virgin Mary. 

Eastwood: Why are you so wary? 

ODB: Wary? 

Eastwood: That's what you said? 

ODB: No, not wary... Mary... I said the Virgin Mary. Her splinter looked like the Virgin Mary. 

Eastwood: Oh. 

ODB: How old are you anyway? 

Eastwood: Too old. 

ODB: The point of it was gonna be that people were praying to my sister's foot. 

Eastwood: Why did they do that? 

ODB: Because her splinter looked religious. All the people in the projects were lining up to pray at my sister's foot. 

Eastwood: Because the splinter was in the shape of the Virgin Mary? 

ODB: Exactly, Clintdog. 

Eastwood: No, I'm vegetarian. 

ODB: Vegetarian? 

Eastwood: No hot dogs for me. 

ODB: I said "Clintdog," not hot dogs. 

Eastwood: Yes, I gave up meat in the spring of '82. 

ODB: That's the same year I quit school. 

Eastwood: It was a good year for me. 

ODB: I got engaged that year to my pastor... a female pastor. 

Eastwood: It did fly by much faster. 

ODB: What? 

Eastwood: For some reason 1982 seems like a blur. It was one of those years that seems a dream now. It was a perfect year for me. 

ODB: I can't believe I'm sitting here with you now. 

Eastwood: Yes. It is strange. 

ODB: You're like an American icon and I'm like the truest American icon. 

Eastwood: Yes, my children love your music. They say you're one of the best. 

ODB: And you know Dirt Dog loves the children? 

Eastwood: Yes. 

ODB: Two American originals... two Frank Sinatras. 

Eastwood: Yes. He had blue eyes. 

h.k. / vice / 2007

Born in Honduras, raised in Guatemala, and eventually exiled to Mexico, Augusto Monterroso (1921-2003) was a deeply respected short story writer. His story "The Dinosaur" is said to be literature's shortest story. In full, it reads: "When he woke up, the dinosaur was still there." His Complete Works and Other Stories (1995) is available in English.
