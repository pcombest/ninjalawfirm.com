STEPHEN CALT

"Rag" songs consisted of two-measure phrases (with the final beat or two beats of each phrase instrumentalized) that were more melodic than blues songs, thanks to their non-keynote cadences. Although the white public had no inkling of the existence of the "rag" idiom, isolated "rag" songs entered mainstream pop music. The chorus of The New Bully, which May Irwin popularized in 1895, was based on a version of Pallet On The Floor called Looking For The Bully Of The Town.
