SEASONAL MUSTACHE

     As the earth travels and turns to point its eager face more directly at the sun, we in the northern hemisphere find ourselves with warmer weather, longer days and shorter skirts.  For a gentleman the coming of spring should be a time to take a sort of personal inventory.  After a long, hard winter he may find that his wardrobe is tired and go out to find a nice pair of trousers.  Or he may glance about at the bare legs along city park pathways and feel a charging need for a little tender companionship.  Of these and many other considerations a gentleman must make as spring blooms, near the top of his list should be his facial hair.

      It goes without saying that a gentleman should sport some arrangement of facial hair.  But what is appropriate for the dark, brutal days of winter is often inappropriate for the hopeful and joyous early days of spring.  For instance, a full beard down past the Adam’s apple is perfect for shielding the face from harsh winter winds, but come June or July is like wearing wool underwear at the beach; awful.  Take this opportunity to try a new style!  Shorten up that beard!!  Let your face see the beautiful sun!!!

      Beards are not the only types of facial hair that benefit from some seasonal pruning.  Moustaches, maybe the most wonderfully subtle of facial hair styles, benefit greatly from a lighter, more summery style.  In my mind, Marcello Mastroianni in “Divorce, Italian Style” sports the perfect summer moustache; trim and clean, it stands out without being showy.  His is just one example of a seasonally appropriate moustache; there are many more to be found on faces you probably already know.

     

Maybe you’ve never worn a moustache but have often thought about it, stealing furtive glances by the nightstand at photos in moustache magazines while your significant other sleeps beside you but thought, “I’m not the type of man to wear a moustache,” or “The style magazines all say that facial hair is déclassé.”  What those “Mad Men” bandwagon jumping, high-rise style magazine writers don’t tell you in the authoritative tone is that they are paid to invent fads.  Every season a new shoe or a new actress or some new hat is “all the rage.”  Some things never go out of style.  Seasonally appropriate, well-maintained facial hair will long outlast the latest reality television show, miracle berry drink or restaurant menu trend.  Now is the time to pull your face up by the handlebars and look your true self right in the mouth.
