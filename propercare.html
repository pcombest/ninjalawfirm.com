by Brendan I. Keegan, Esq.

 

The term gentleman is thrown around like a rapscallion’s baseball these days.  Tossed hither and thither, it gathers dirt and bits of debris until eventually it breaks a window in some abandoned factory and is left to gather dust until such day that an enterprising urban developer sees fit to demolish the structure, burying the baseball under hundreds of pounds of brick, never to be thrown again.

It is therefore my principal interest to rescue this term from its purgatory of overuse and misconstruance and restore the word to its rightful place of distinction in the lexicon.  Henceforth, this space will provide tips for the young man interested in fulfilling his true potential as a 21st century gentleman.
